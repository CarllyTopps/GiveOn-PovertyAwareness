Understanding Poverty

What is Poverty?

Poverty is a complex social issue that affects millions of people worldwide. It's not just about lacking money—it's about lacking access to basic human needs like food, clean water, healthcare, education, and shelter.

Types of Poverty

  • Absolute Poverty: Living below the international poverty line of $2.15 per day
  • Relative Poverty: Living below 50% of the median income in one's country
  • Multidimensional Poverty: Lacking access to multiple basic needs simultaneously

Root Causes

Economic Factors

Unemployment, low wages, economic inequality, and lack of economic opportunities

Social Factors

Discrimination, social exclusion, lack of education, and limited social mobility

Environmental Factors

Natural disasters, climate change, and environmental degradation

Political Factors

Corruption, poor governance, conflict, and lack of political stability
