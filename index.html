Understanding Poverty

What is Poverty?

Poverty is a complex social issue that affects millions of people worldwide. It's not just about lacking money—it's about lacking access to basic human needs like food, clean water, healthcare, education, and shelter. Poverty manifests in various forms and affects individuals, families, and entire communities across different dimensions of life.

The World Bank defines extreme poverty as living on less than $2.15 per day (2017 PPP), but poverty encompasses much more than just income. It includes lack of access to education, healthcare, clean water, sanitation, adequate housing, and social protection. The multidimensional nature of poverty means that addressing it requires comprehensive approaches that go beyond simple income transfers.

Types of Poverty

  • Absolute Poverty: Living below the international poverty line of $2.15 per day, unable to meet basic survival needs
  • Relative Poverty: Living below 50% of the median income in one's country, experiencing social exclusion
  • Multidimensional Poverty: Lacking access to multiple basic needs simultaneously across health, education, and living standards
  • Urban Poverty: Concentrated in cities with inadequate housing, services, and employment opportunities
  • Rural Poverty: Affecting agricultural communities with limited infrastructure and market access
  • Child Poverty: Affecting children's development, education, and future opportunities

Global Impact of Poverty

Poverty has far-reaching consequences that extend beyond individual suffering. It affects economic growth, social stability, health outcomes, and environmental sustainability. Children growing up in poverty face significant barriers to education and development, perpetuating cycles of disadvantage across generations. Communities with high poverty rates often experience increased crime, reduced social cohesion, and limited economic opportunities.

Root Causes

Economic Factors

Unemployment, low wages, economic inequality, lack of economic opportunities, and unstable employment markets

Social Factors

Discrimination, social exclusion, lack of education, limited social mobility, and cultural barriers

Environmental Factors

Natural disasters, climate change, environmental degradation, and resource depletion

Political Factors

Corruption, poor governance, conflict, lack of political stability, and inadequate policy frameworks

Key Insights

  • Over 80% of the world's extreme poor live in rural areas
  • Children under 18 represent 50% of those living in extreme poverty
  • Women and girls are disproportionately affected by poverty
  • Climate change threatens to push 100+ million people into poverty by 2030

Progress Made

  • Extreme poverty has declined from 36% in 1990 to 9.2% in 2023
  • China lifted 800+ million people out of poverty since 1980
  • Global middle class has grown from 1.8 billion to 3.2 billion people
  • Child mortality rates have decreased by 60% since 1990

Pathways to Solutions

Education & Skills

Investing in quality education and vocational training to break the cycle of poverty

  • Universal primary education with focus on quality learning outcomes
  • Adult literacy programs and lifelong learning opportunities
  • Technical and vocational training aligned with market needs
  • Digital skills development for the modern economy
  • STEM education to prepare for future job markets
  • Financial literacy programs for better money management

Economic Opportunities

Creating sustainable employment and entrepreneurship opportunities

  • Microfinance programs with flexible repayment terms
  • Small business development and mentorship programs
  • Fair trade initiatives ensuring fair wages
  • Rural development projects and agricultural support
  • Job creation through infrastructure development
  • Support for women entrepreneurs and gender equality

Healthcare Access

Ensuring universal access to quality healthcare services

  • Universal health coverage and insurance schemes
  • Maternal and child health programs
  • Disease prevention and vaccination campaigns
  • Mental health support and counseling services
  • Mobile health clinics for remote areas
  • Community health worker training programs

Basic Needs

Providing access to essential services and infrastructure

  • Clean water and sanitation infrastructure
  • Affordable housing and slum upgrading programs
  • Food security programs and nutrition support
  • Energy access through renewable sources
  • Transportation infrastructure for better connectivity
  • Internet access for digital inclusion

Success Stories & Case Studies

Rwanda's Education Revolution

Rwanda has achieved remarkable progress in education, with primary school enrollment reaching 98% and literacy rates increasing from 58% in 1991 to 73% in 2018. The country's focus on quality education and teacher training has created a foundation for economic growth and poverty reduction.

98% Primary Enrollment 73% Literacy Rate

Bangladesh's Microfinance Impact

Grameen Bank and other microfinance institutions have helped millions of women in Bangladesh start small businesses, improving their families' economic status and contributing to the country's significant poverty reduction from 44% in 1991 to 14% in 2016.

9M+ Women Served 14% Poverty Rate

Brazil's Bolsa Família Program

Brazil's conditional cash transfer program has lifted millions out of poverty by providing financial assistance to families who keep their children in school and ensure regular health check-ups. The program has been credited with reducing poverty by 25% and extreme poverty by 50%.

14M+ Families 25% Poverty Reduction

Latest News & Updates

Featured

Estimated 272 Million Children & Youth Are Out of School Globally

New estimates from UNESCO UIS and the Global Education Monitoring Report show that about 272 million children and youth were out of school in 2023, which is 21 million more than previously thought. This rise reflects updated population data and attendance reports, underscoring major challenges in achieving universal education. Many of those out of school live in conflict-affected, rural, or low-income areas where access remains limited. Girls, especially in sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia, remain disproportionately affected. The report stresses the urgent need for investment in inclusive education systems to close these gaps. Without stronger global action, millions risk being permanently excluded from learning opportunities.

June 9, 2025 UIS / GEM / World Education Blog
Survey / Philippines

63% of Filipino Households Self-Rate as Poor, Highest in 21 Years

According to the Social Weather Stations (SWS), about 63% of Filipino families consider themselves poor as of December 2024, marking the highest self-rated poverty level in over two decades. Food poverty among families has also increased, particularly in Visayas and Mindanao.

January 8, 2025 Philstar.com / SWS
Government / Philippines

Almost 2.5M Filipinos Lifted Out of Poverty as Rate Falls to 15.5%

The Philippines saw a decrease in poverty incidence to 15.5% in 2023 (from 18.1% in 2021), which translates to nearly 2.5 million Filipinos lifted above the poverty line. This figure is now below pre-pandemic levels. Officials highlight that sustained job creation and targeted social programs played a key role in driving this progress.

July 22, 2024 PCO Philippines
Development / Philippines

Government Expects Poverty Incidence to Fall to as Low as 8% by 2028

Under the updated Philippine Development Plan (2023-2028), the government aims to reduce poverty incidence to single-digit levels by 2028. They are targeting 10-11% by 2027 and 8-9% by 2028, building on recent declines from 18.1% in 2021 to 15.5% in 2023.

August 18, 2025 BusinessWorld Online
Global Policy

World Bank Raises Global Poverty Line to $3/Day, Revising Estimates Upward

In June 2025, the World Bank updated the international poverty line from $2.15 to $3.00 per day (based on 2021 PPPs), which increases the estimated number of people in extreme poverty and shows how sensitive poverty metrics are to changes in purchasing power worldwide.

June 5, 2025 World Bank
Food Security / Global

Global Hunger Declines Slightly, But Conflict & Climate Threaten Future Gains

A UN report says global hunger dropped to 8.2% of the population in 2024, down from 8.5% in 2023. But conflict, climate shocks, and rising costs could reverse the trend. Africa remains especially vulnerable, with over 307 million people chronically undernourished. The study warns that without urgent action, recent progress could quickly be undone.

July 28, 2025 Reuters / UN SOFI Report
Global Policy

Estimate of People Living in Extreme Poverty Rises to 808 Million Globally in 2025

New data shows that under the updated $3.00/day international poverty line, approximately 808 million people — nearly 1 in 10 globally — were living in extreme poverty in 2025. The revised line reflects updated purchasing power parity adjustments and newer survey data. This change underscores how many more are affected when measurement thresholds are modernized.

June 5, 2025 UN SDG Statistics
Food Security

Global Hunger Declines Slightly to 8.2%, But Africa and Western Asia Still Struggle

According to the SOFI 2025 report, around 8.2% of the global population (≈ 673 million people) experienced hunger in 2024, down from 8.5% in 2023. However, hunger continued to increase in many subregions of Africa and Western Asia, even as southern Asia and Latin America saw improvements. The report emphasizes that targeted policies are needed to protect the most vulnerable.

July 28, 2025 WHO / UN SOFI Report
